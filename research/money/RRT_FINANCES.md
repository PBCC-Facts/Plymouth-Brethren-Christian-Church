## Money in

- UK FY2024: income £3,516,913 (donations/legacies £3.32m; investment income
  £138.9k; government grants £30k).
- US FY2025: revenue $3,031,086, of which contributions $1.50m (49.4%) and
  **investment income $1,509,208 (49.8%)**; net assets $4.78m. A young
  disaster-relief charity earning half its revenue from investments is
  unusual.
- NZ FYE Dec 2025: gross income NZ$3,765,403; expenditure NZ$3,686,173.
- Australia: real figures are in the ACNC AIS (fetch timed out; gap).
- The Grace Trust (UK 257516) made a **£1.085m grant to RRT in 2024**
  (related-party note in Grace Trust accounts; see VISION_FUND.md).
- Who ultimately funds RRT (members vs UBT vs church trusts) is not
  disclosed on any register page loaded.

## Money out / related-party

- **US Schedule L flag (primary):** the FY2025 Form 990 reports
  conflict-of-interest transactions requiring Schedule L disclosure
  (ProPublica summary). The Schedule L detail pages are the single
  highest-value unfetched document.
- UK: two loans totalling **£200,000 to Rapid Relief Team Trading Limited**
  (wholly-owned subsidiary, same trustees as directors), base + 2%,
  repayable June 2023 (filed accounts).
- 2018 drought appeal: SMH, 21 Aug 2018, reported nearly half of 2016-17
  appeal funds went to internal expenses despite the "100 per cent to
  farmers" claim (cited via Wikipedia; direct SMH copy still needed).
- Brethren Intelligence (tier c) alleges RRT invested in a business owned by
  a UK Brethren leader's family while he served as trustee:
  https://www.brethrenintelligence.com/rapid-relief-team-conflict-of-interest.html
- Truck/trailer/consumables ownership and supplier chain (UBT? Campus & Co?):
  not established. Gap.

## Regulator / institutional scrutiny

- The 2014 Preston Down Trust decision conditioned PBCC charitable status on
  demonstrated public benefit "including through disaster relief": the
  founding context for RRT (2013).
- SA Health withdrew collaboration with RRT, Nov 2024, over PBCC affiliation
  (ABC News, 20 Nov 2024, via Wikipedia).
- No formal ACNC or Charity Commission inquiry into RRT found.
