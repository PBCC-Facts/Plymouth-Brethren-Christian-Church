## Headline

**"Vision Foundation" is named in The Grace Trust's own audited accounts filed
with the UK Charity Commission** (charity no. 257516). Filings archived in
`research/money/filings/grace-trust-257516-accounts-fy20{20..24}.pdf`.

Overview page (verified): https://register-of-charities.charitycommission.gov.uk/en/charity-search/-/charity-details/257516/full-print

## What the filings literally say (tier a, primary)

1. Funds note (FY2020, FY2023, FY2024, identical wording): "Vision Foundation -
   this expendable endowment fund represents funds which have arisen from a
   fundraising drive by the charity. These are ring-fenced funds which will be
   invested to create further fundraising returns in the future. At present the
   investment policy is being considered by the Trustees." The "policy being
   considered" line has been repeated for at least five years.
2. Endowment balance: £7.42m (31 Dec 2020) → £21.39m (1 Jan 2023) → £47.26m
   (31 Dec 2023, after £7.09m new income and £18.77m investment gains) →
   **£50.16m (31 Dec 2024)**.
3. **Vision Global Growth Fund**: £6.5m invested at 31 Dec 2019 "to facilitate
   equity investment in a manner consistent with the ethical principles of the
   trust"; written down £500k in 2020; revalued **+£18,227,270 in 2023 alone**
   to £24.73m; £27.04m at 31 Dec 2024. Unlisted; no manager or domicile stated.
4. **Vision Investment Fund**: £15m invested (FY2023 accounts say at
   31 Dec 2022; FY2024 accounts say at 31 Dec 2023 — the filings contradict
   each other); valued £15.44m end-2023; **disposed during 2024 for £15.59m**.
   Buyer not stated.
5. Context: Grace Trust income peaked £127-130m/yr (2020-21), £60.7m in 2024.
   Corporate trustees **Aller Brook Limited** and **Scribefort Limited**;
   accounts signed by Joseph Brewer, director of both. Related-party note shows
   a £1.085m grant to Rapid Relief Team in 2024.
