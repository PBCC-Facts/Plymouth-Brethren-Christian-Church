# Launch posts. The Facts on LinkedIn

Short posts, one idea each. Register per EDITORIAL_GUIDE.md still applies: documented practice, named sources, no adjectives doing the work, no em-dashes. Link in the last line. One post every 3-4 days.

## Hashtags

LinkedIn rewards 3-5 hashtags. Two jobs: reach members (they follow their own institutions' tags) and own the search terms.

**Every post gets these three:**
`#PlymouthBrethrenChristianChurch #PlymouthBrethren #ExclusiveBrethren`

**Rotate 1-2 of these to land in member feeds** (tags the church's own orgs post under):
`#UBT #OneSchoolGlobal #RRT #RapidReliefTeam #RRTcares`

## Tagging (@mentions)

Tag the institutions, never individuals. Mentioning a company page notifies its admins and surfaces the post to people who engage; these are the pages Brethren actually follow:

| Tag | Page | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| @UBT | linkedin.com/company/ubt-global | The church's business network; nearly every Brethren business owner follows it |
| @OneSchool Global | linkedin.com/company/oneschoolglobal (regional: NZ, UK, Australia pages exist) | Staff and parents |
| @Rapid Relief Team | regional pages: rapid-relief-team-ltd-rrt- (AU, 22k followers), rapid-relief-team-rrt-united-kingdom (13k), rapidreliefteamus, rapidreliefteamca, rapid-relief-team-rrt-new-zealand | The church's public face; largest Brethren followings on LinkedIn |

Use tags where the post is about that institution (money post → @UBT; schools claim → @OneSchool Global). Don't stack all of them on every post: one or two per post reads as documentation, five reads as spam and LinkedIn suppresses it.

**Never tag:** individual members, ex-members, survivors, or journalists without their explicit OK. Tagging a survivor outs them to their family's network. The church has no official company page to tag; only the orgs above.
