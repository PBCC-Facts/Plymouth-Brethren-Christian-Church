## About (Overview, 2000 max)

The Facts is an independent, open-source record of the Plymouth Brethren Christian Church, the group formerly known as the Exclusive Brethren.

The original reporting happens elsewhere: ABC Four Corners, The Times, Stuff NZ, Guardian Australia, Byline Times, the New Zealand Royal Commission, the UK Charity Commission, and the Australian Taxation Office have all put findings about the PBCC on the public record. Survivor-run archives like PBCCstories.org and the Get A Life podcast have done the same. What has been missing is a library that organises all of it in one place, on the same search terms the church itself uses.

That is what this project is. Every claim on the site carries a citation to the journalist, court, regulator, or named survivor who put it on record first. Where a source is still being verified, the site says so visibly. Nothing is published silently.

The project is open source on GitHub. Researchers, journalists, ex-members, and current members are welcome to file corrections, contribute new public-record evidence, or challenge any claim through a public issue. Corrections make the record better.

First-person testimony from people who grew up in or left the fellowship is published only with explicit written consent, reviewed by the contributor before it ships, and removable on request.

The Facts is not affiliated with the Plymouth Brethren Christian Church.

Read the record: https://pbccfacts.com
Contribute or correct: https://github.com/PBCC-Facts

## Notes for the audience this page is for

Members can use LinkedIn but little else. That means this page is often the first non-church account of the PBCC a member will ever scroll past. Register rules apply harder here than anywhere: documented practice, no adjectives, no mockery, every post names its source. A member should be able to read any post and find nothing to dismiss it with except the record itself.
