# Launch posts. The Facts on X

X is a different audience from LinkedIn. Members of the PBCC can only use LinkedIn, so X is **not** where you reach them. X reaches journalists, researchers, ex-members, and the general public, and it is where amplification and backlinks come from. So the strategy inverts:

- **LinkedIn:** tag the church's own institutions (UBT, OneSchool, RRT) to land in member feeds. Never tag journalists or survivors.
- **X:** tag the **outlets and reporters** who did the original work. They may repost, which is the whole game here. Still never tag survivors or ex-members without their explicit OK.

Register is unchanged (EDITORIAL_GUIDE.md): documented practice, named sources, no adjectives doing the work, no em-dashes.

## Hashtags on X

X culture punishes hashtag stacking. Use **one, at most two**, and only real ones people actually search:
`#ExclusiveBrethren` (higher search volume on X than the rebrand name). Optionally add `#PlymouthBrethren`. Drop them entirely on the reply tweets in a thread.

## Tagging: handles to verify before posting

Confidence noted honestly. **Confirm the ones marked (verify) by opening the profile before you hit post** — tagging a dead or wrong handle notifies a stranger and wastes the mention.

| Handle | Who | Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `@4corners` | ABC Four Corners | Real, but the account was archived Aug 2023. Prefer `@ABCaustralia` or `@abcnews` for a live account. |
| `@thetimes` | The Times (UK) | Confident |
| `@guardian` / `@GuardianAus` | Guardian / Guardian Australia | Confident |
| `@BylineTimes` | Byline Times | (verify) |
| `@michelle_duff` | Michelle Duff, Stuff/Post NZ reporter on the Brethren | Confirmed handle; confirm she covered the specific claim before crediting her |
| Stuff NZ / The Post | Stuff rebranded; The Post (thepost.co.nz) is now separate | (verify current handle before tagging) |

Rule: only tag an outlet on a post that actually cites that outlet's reporting. Tag in the **reply/quote or the last line**, not the first, so the tag does not gate the tweet's reach.

A thread is the strongest launch format on X. Post 1/6 with the launch card image, then reply down the chain.

The website is live: pbccfacts.com

An independent, open-source record of the Plymouth Brethren Christian Church, formerly the Exclusive Brethren.

One citation per claim. 🧵

**2/**
It contains no original reporting. That already exists.

ABC Four Corners, The Times, Stuff NZ, Guardian Australia, Byline Times, the NZ Royal Commission, the UK Charity Commission, the ATO. Two decades of it.

**3/**
The problem was never a shortage of evidence. It was that the evidence lived in twenty different places, behind paywalls and case numbers.

Search the church's name and you mostly find the church.

**4/**
So this is a library, not a publication. Every claim links to the outlet, court, or regulator that put it on record first. Claims still being verified are marked as pending. Nothing is asserted silently.

**5/**

**6/**
It is open source. If a claim is wrong, the repo is public and the issue tracker is open. That invitation includes the church itself.

Read the record: pbccfacts.com
Contribute: github.com/PBCC-Facts
