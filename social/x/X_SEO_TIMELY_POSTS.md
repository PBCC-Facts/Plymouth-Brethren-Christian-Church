# Timely X threads, with teeth

Goal: when a Brethren story is moving and someone searches to find out what's going on, one of these threads is what they find, and it doesn't pull punches. The hit comes from the documented facts, led hard and named, not from adjectives. Register per EDITORIAL_GUIDE.md §1.4: "plainspoken **and** unsoftened."

**How these are written:**
- **Lead with the sharpest sourced fact,** not a curiosity hook. The first tweet should make a stranger stop scrolling.
- **No false balance.** Serious reporting and a PR denial are not two equal sides. State what's documented; the church's response gets one attributed line, not the last word.
- **Name the subject:** the fellowship, its leadership, Bruce D. Hales, UBT. Never a survivor (§1.6).
- **Attribute everything** (§1.2). "ABC Four Corners reported," "the UK Charity Commission found." The teeth are legal precisely because every claim is sourced.
- **No adjectives doing the work** (§4). Delete "sinister," "shocking," "brainwashed." The rat-poison quote needs no adjective; it is the adjective.

> Verification: facts marked ✅ are on file in FACTS.md (Hales 2015 arsenic remark, Doctrine of Separation, ATO raid, Albanese/Rudd designations). The Australian-election recordings specifics are contested; that thread leads on the *documented* pattern of Brethren political campaigning and attributes the new reporting without asserting its contested details. ## Operating notes

- **Post the news threads the day a story moves.** Thread 1 is timely only while the story is live.
- **Attribute, never assert a contested claim in your own voice.** The teeth stay legal because the facts are sourced. That is the entire posture: strident loses, sourced wins.
- **Never soften with adjectives and never sharpen with them.** Both fail the self-test. If deleting every adjective changes the force of the tweet, rewrite it around a harder fact instead.
- **Tag the outlet you cite** (handle table in X_LAUNCH_POSTS.md), in the last tweet. Never tag survivors or ex-members.
- **The subject of every sharp sentence is the church, its leaders, or its institutions.** Never a member or a survivor.
- **Fill every `[SOURCE: …]` before posting.** A sharp claim without its link is the one that gets you sued; with the link it's journalism.
